Common Vision Problems Children Experience

Children face unique vision concerns as their eyes grow and develop. We treat many pediatric eye diseases and conditions here at our NY Queens Eye Center. Our comprehensive eye exam takes into consideration several factors and eye components to make sure your child's vision is at 100%. Here are some signs and symptoms of vision problems in children:

  • Squinting.

  • Poor reading or writing skills.

  • Frequent headaches.

  • Inability to focus on subject matter.

  • The child's eyes are not straight.

Signs of Vision Problems in Children

For many parents, spotting signs of eye concerns in their kids is not easy. Children are excellent at compensating for vision problems, and signs in toddlers and preschoolers are often hard to see. Some common early symptoms of vision problems include: poor fine motor skills, inability to do simple puzzles, inability to focus on pictures in a book, frequent headaches, rubbing of the eyes, sensitivity to light, squinting, tilting the head, crossing eyes, etc. Most common cases of eyes problems in children are

  • Refractive error
  • Astigmatism
  • Near sightedness
  • Far sightedness
  • Amblyopia ( Lazy Eye )
  • Poor coordination between eyes
  • Poor hand and eye coordination
