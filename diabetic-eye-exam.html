Diabetic Eye Exam in Queens, NY

Diabetes has far-reaching implications throughout your body, and your eyes are one area that can be affected. Diabetics are at higher risk for serious eye conditions, such as glaucoma and cataracts. They are also at risk of developing a condition called diabetic retinopathy. Diabetic eye exams are critical for protection for diabetic patients, helping them take control of their eye health and protect their vision from these serious conditions.Book an Appointment now at Sutphin Eye care for you all entire family.

What to Expect at a Diabetic Eye Exam

A diabetic eye exam is an eye exam designed to detect diabetic eye problems, including diabetic retinopathy. This exam involves dilation of the eyes, which gives the doctor the ability to see the retina and examine them for any signs of retinopathy.

A diabetic eye exam can be performed by either an optometrist or an ophthalmologist. However, if diabetic eye disease is found, an ophthalmologist will take over care to provide treatment. Having the right eye doctor assisting you with your diabetic eye care treatment is critical to preventing the progression of diabetic eye disease.Before symptoms develop, only an eye doctor can identify and diagnose the condition and help you control it.

You should have your first diabetic eye exam not long after your diagnosis of diabetes. Because diabetes often has gone undetected or years, it’s possible that you are already suffering damage. After your first exam, you will need to have an eye exam every year to ensure you are not suffering from retinopathy. Discuss the recommended frequency with your doctor when you come in for your exam.

What Are the Signs of Diabetic Retinopathy?

One of the challenges with diagnosing diabetic retinopathy is the fact that in the early stages, it causes no symptoms. As it progresses, however, it causes serious, permanent damage to the eyes and can lead to blindness. Some of the early symptoms to watch for include:

  • Appearance of eye floaters
  • Dark, empty areas in the field of vision
  • Blurred vision
  • Focusing problems
  • Total or partial vision loss

If diabetic retinopathy is discovered at your diabetic eye exam, the Houston Eye Doctor team will create a list of recommendations to stop the progression of the disease.
