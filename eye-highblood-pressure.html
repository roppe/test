Our optometrists treat each patient with the utmost care and compassion, a quality that puts us head and shoulders above the rest.For many people, contact lenses provide greater convenience and more satisfying vision correction than eyeglasses. Here’s what’s involved in a typical contact lens exam and fitting:

What Happens During a Contact Lens Exam?
  • Visual acuity tests using a projected eye chart to assess the clarity of your near and far vision
  • Eye movement tests to see how rapidly and easily your eyes track to different points
  • Cover tests to assess the individual strength of each eye
  • Depth perception checks to see if your eyes move in tandem
  • Tests designed to determine the health of corneas, irises, eyelids, and more
Tou may be able to choose from the following lenses:
  • Daily disposables
  • Lenses for astigmatism
  • Bifocal lenses
  • Colored lenses
  • Pediatric lenses
  • Rigid gas permeable lenses
  • More

It’s important to keep your follow-up exam so that your optometrist can assess how well your lenses fit.
