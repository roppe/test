Contact Lens Exams

Whether you’re ready to give contact lenses a try for the first time or have been wearing them for years, Queens Eye Center is your one-stop shop for comprehensive contact lens exams and a wide variety of contact lenses designed for your unique vision needs. At Sutphin Eye Care Queens Eye Center, our experienced doctor and staff will put you on the road to better vision by conducting a thorough eye examination designed to not only correct your vision but also assess the overall health of your eyes. If you need a contact lens exam in New York, Contact Sutphin Eye Care for an appointment.

What Happens During a Contact Lens Exam?

Why should you choose Sutphin Eye Care for your contact lenses needs? When you do, you will enjoy several major benefits that set us apart from other family eye care clinics. A contact lens exam begins with a comprehensive eye exam, which includes:

  • Measuring the curvature of your cornea

  • Very detailed refraction to find the best vision

  • Conversion of refraction to contact lens perscription

  • Finding the correct base curve and diameter

  • Observation of the tri lenses while on the eyes to ensure optimum fit

  • Follow up exam after dispensing trial contact lenses to ensure optimum comfort, vision and fit.

We also offer a large selection of Eyewear and Vision products. We have selected our collection of high-quality frames to reflect the latest in eye wear style, fashion and functionality.

If you need a contact lens exam Call Us for Appointment at 718-739-2755 or 718-739-8939 or Contact Us Online

Here are some examples of different types of contact lenses we work with on a daily basis:

  • Soft lenses
  • Bifocal contact lenses (for optimum distance and near vision)
  • Lenses for Astigmatism
  • Colored
  • Rigid gas permeable
  • Speciality and medically necessary lenses ( such as lenses for keratoconus)

Once your contact lenses are in place, our optometrist will advise you to wear them as prescribed for several days to a week so you can decide how you like them and if they’re comfortable. We’ll make a follow-up appointment to address your concerns, if any, or try another pair if you’re not happy.

It’s important to follow up with your exams so that our optometrist can assess how well your lenses fit you. Contact lenses that are the wrong size or not worn as directed can damage your eyes.
